Introduction

Waste management is the process of handling waste from its inception to its final disposal. This includes collection, transport, treatment, and disposal of waste.

Types of Waste

  • Biodegradable Waste – food waste, garden waste
  • Non-Biodegradable Waste – plastics, metals
  • Hazardous Waste – chemicals, batteries
  • Electronic Waste – old computers, phones

Importance of Waste Management

Proper waste management helps protect the environment, reduces pollution, and promotes health and well-being for communities. It also conserves resources and reduces greenhouse gas emissions.

Best Practices

  • Segregate waste at the source
  • Compost organic waste
  • Recycle paper, plastic, and glass
  • Avoid single-use plastics
  • Participate in community clean-up efforts
